Walking Hurts. Skating Lets Me Live.

I have a pelvic disability that makes ordinary walking painful and physiologically stressful. Inline skates work as a prosthetic adaptation: they let me move, work, drive, use transit, and enter public spaces with less impact.

FSI, the Fractal Stability Index, is patent-pending technology for understanding stability and stress from real-world movement data. This site explains the public case without exposing proprietary formulas.

The simple version

This is not recreation. It is a mobility solution built from lived necessity, court and agency records, video evidence, and wearable data.

Function

Skates replace painful steps

Walking creates impact and pelvic torsion. Skating changes the movement pattern, reduces repeated vertical shock, and makes daily transportation possible.

Why this matters to Google Fitbit and Health teams

This is a real-world health AI case study: accessibility, wearable sensing, human movement, physiological stress, product ethics, and model design all meet in one documented problem.

Product relevance

Wearables can help people prove invisible disability, detect unsafe access barriers, and personalize mobility recommendations without reducing disability to generic step counts.

Research relevance

The data challenge includes noisy real-world labels, route context, activity classification, HR/HRV features, fairness, explainability, and privacy-preserving health insights.
